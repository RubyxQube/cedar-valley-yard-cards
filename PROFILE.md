**Cedar Valley Yard Cards' advantage over franchises:** They are local, independent, and personal - no franchise fees, no corporate feel. A good website with real photos and easy booking would let them compete directly.

## Recommended Aesthetic Direction for `/frontend-design`

**Suggested direction:** Bright celebration aesthetic - warm, joyful, community-forward. Think confetti colors but tasteful: white base with bold pops of color (coral, sky blue, yellow-gold), rounded typography, generous use of real photo/yard setup imagery. The feel should be "your neighbor who throws great parties" - approachable, fun, zero corporate edge. Clean and modern enough to build trust, playful enough to match the product.

**Why:** The product is inherently emotional and visual. People booking yard cards are celebrating someone they love. The site needs to instantly feel like a celebration, not a rental company. The brand tone from their posts is warm and personal - the site should reflect that.

**Accent color direction:** Warm coral or celebration rose (#e85d75 range) as primary accent, with sky blue (#5bbcd1 range) as a secondary pop. White base. Gold/yellow for number/letter card elements in imagery. Avoid the typical "party supply" neon palette - keep it elevated and photo-forward.

**Font feel:** Rounded but clean. A display serif or soft sans-serif for headlines (something like Playfair Display or DM Serif Display for occasion/celebration feel), clean geometric sans for body (DM Sans or similar). Not script fonts - those read as amateur in this industry.
